\noindent \textred{2.} Use the substitution method to prove that,
$$ T(n) = 2T(\frac{n}{2}) + c n \log n $$
is $O(n(\log  n)^2)$, where $c > 0$ is a constant. ($\log \equiv \log 2$, in this and the following questions)
\newline \\
\textblue{
Assume $T(k) \leq c_1 \cdot k(\log  k)^2, ~\forall k < n$, we have
\[
\begin{aligned}
    T(n) &= 2T(\frac{n}{2}) + c n~\log  n \\
    &\leq 2c_1 \cdot \frac{n}{2} (\log \frac{n}{2})^2  + c \cdot n~\log  n \\
    &= c_1 \cdot n (\log  n - 1)^2 + c \cdot n~\log  n \\
    &= c_1 \cdot n (\log  n)^2 + (c - 2c_1) \cdot n~\log  n + c_1 \cdot n = \underline{O(n(\log  n)^2)}
\end{aligned}
\]
